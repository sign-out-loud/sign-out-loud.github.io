Sign Out Loud


Giving a voice to those without.

What are we doing?


Using modern video processing technology we are enabling communication between those who cannot speak and those who cannot use sign language. We will take a real-time video of a sign and do a translation to the english word that was spoken.

Why are we doing it?


In the past 30 years technology has advanced to give a voice to the deaf and mute. Currently, the system makes instantaneous communication quite difficult for anyone who does not know sign language. It is a cumbersome system that slows communication. We want to fix that by creating a system that allows for signs to translate directly to spoken communication.

This will open up real-time communication between those who do not know sign language and those who are unable to speak in any other way. Extended uses of this could include using voice activated programs with sign language, opening up a whole new method of interacting with technology.

How are we going to do it?


Using a video camera, we will capture the user's sign language inputs over video. We will then send them to our back end computer vision processing software. This software will recognize the shapes of a user's hands, translate it to a word, and then output the spoken-word translation to the other end users.
